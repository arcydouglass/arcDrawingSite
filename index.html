My work is an exploration to explore ways to generate the most amount of complexity in a space with the smallest number of parts. I use very simple geometric shapes like squares, triangles, hexagons, circles and arcs. I use fragments of the shapes and recombine the shapes according to an external source like the first thousand digits of pi. The shapes create a complex, dynamic space that share some of the variety and richness that I see in natural systems.

Field

Field is an a project based on a field of very simple geometric shapes that are determined by the digits of pi. Each digits generates a specific orientation of a ninety degree arc. The arcs are overlaid to create unique fields of patterns. The line thickness of the arcs are determined by the number of arcs that are superimposed on top of one another.

Field can also exist in color
